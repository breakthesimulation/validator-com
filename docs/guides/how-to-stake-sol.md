# How to Stake SOL in 2026

Staking Solana (SOL) is one of the most effective ways to earn passive income while supporting network security. This guide walks you through the entire process.

## What is Solana Staking?

Solana staking involves locking your SOL tokens to support the network's consensus mechanism. In return, you earn staking rewards (APY). The current average APY is around 6-7% for most validators.

## Prerequisites

- A Solana wallet (Phantom, Solflare, Ledger, or Torus)
- SOL tokens to stake (minimum 0.01 SOL recommended)
- Some SOL for transaction fees (around 0.0001 SOL per transaction)

## Step-by-Step Staking Guide

### Step 1: Choose Your Wallet

| Wallet | Best For | Security |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Phantom | Browser extension, DeFi | High |
| Solflare | Staking-focused, mobile | High |
| Ledger | Hardware security | Very High |
| Sollet | Web wallet | Medium |

**We recommend Phantom or Solflare for most users.**

### Step 2: Acquire SOL

If you don't already have SOL:

1. Buy from exchanges (Binance, Coinbase, Kraken)
2. Transfer to your staking wallet
3. Wait for confirmation (usually 1-2 blocks)

### Step 3: Choose a Validator

Consider these factors:

- **Commission**: 0% commission maximizes your rewards
- **Uptime**: Look for 99.9% or higher
- **Jito MEV**: Validators with Jito earn additional MEV rewards
- **Stake size**: Very large validators may impact decentralization

**Avoid validators with:**
- Commission >10%
- Uptime <99%
- Delinquent status

### Step 4: Stake Your SOL

Using Phantom wallet:

1. Open Phantom and click "Stake"
2. Click "Stake with a new validator"
3. Search for your chosen validator (e.g., "ParaFi Technologies")
4. Enter the amount of SOL to stake
5. Review and confirm the transaction
6. Wait for confirmation (~30 seconds)

### Step 5: Monitor Your Rewards

After staking:

- Rewards begin accruing immediately
- Claimable after ~1-2 epochs (~2-4 days)
- Check your wallet's staking dashboard

## Understanding Validator Metrics

| Metric | What It Means | Good Value |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| Commission | Validator's fee | 0-5% |
| Stake Amount | Total SOL delegated | Any |
| APY | Annual reward rate | 5-7% |
| Uptime | Network participation | 99.9%+ |
| Jito | MEV reward sharing | Yes preferred |

## Maximizing Your Staking Rewards

1. **Choose 0% commission validators** - More money for you
2. **Enable Jito** - Additional 1-3% MEV rewards
3. **Re-stake rewards** - Compound your returns
4. **Consider multiple validators** - Decentralize the network

## Common Staking Questions

### How long do rewards take to appear?
Rewards are locked for ~2 epochs (~2-4 days) before becoming claimable.

### Can I unstake anytime?
Yes, but there's a 2-3 epoch cooldown period (~1-2 days) before tokens become liquid.

### What happens if the validator goes down?
You don't lose your SOL. However, you won't earn rewards during downtime. The validator's uptime metric will reflect this.

### Is staking risky?
Staking is one of the lower-risk ways to earn crypto rewards. Your principal is safe unless the validator acts maliciously (rare).
